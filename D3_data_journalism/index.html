Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks & Income

As the median household income increases, the percent of obesity in the population generally decreases. Similarly, as the median income increases, the percentage of the population that smokes decreases.

Conversely, as the percentage of the population in poverty increases, the percentage of obese people, and of smokers, increases.

As always, correlation does not mean causation. However, these trends merit more exploration into ways that health risks such as smoking and obesity could be reduced among poorer communities.
